June 2023

I started really coding in June 2023. I had experience with electives in high school but a really unfortunate situation in college caused me to drop CS. Ultimately, I really wanted to make a video game for a YouTube video and decided to teach myself C# and Unity using these tutorials from Brackeys.

June 2023.2

It turns out, I really love development and making sense of things on my own. I was so enthralled by coding and working on this first game, that I finished learning the basics and making the game in just 7 days. It turned into a great hook for the YouTube video.

July 2023

Immediately, I got to work on my next game, an extended cut of Pong, the original video game. I also themed the game around Jerma (again), as I had specific visual gag in mind for the playing field. Was it overkill to make Pong in Unity? Sure, but at this point, I'm just learning as much as I can about this massive, ever-growing space. This was also a fun video to make

August 2023

At this point, it was time to up the ante and learn something completely new. In the span of a month, I learned HTML, CSS, JavaScript for the frontend of an advanced task manager app called "Browser Stickies" . In this same month, I learned the fundamentals of building my own REST API using Express.js/Node and MongoDB to persist user data and how to deploy an app. Phew.

September 2023

Constantly learning is extremely important to me, so I decided to dig deeper into algorithms and data structures. My education in this area of software engineering is far from complete but I believe this freeCodeCamp certification has given me a solid foundation, which is crucial in moving forward.

September 2023.2

For my next certification, I turned to responsive web design. One of the projects required for completion was to create a product landing page. I took it a bit further and built a site for an entire fake brand. Lux Lane is a line of luxury gaming peripherals and, from a design standpoint, is one of my cleanest projects. Of course, there are areas to improve upon and details that still bug me, but overall, I'm proud of it!

September 2023.3

Having finished my responsive web design certification, I feel a lot more confident now in my ability to build a website/webapp from scratch—adjusting, upgrading, and evolving it as I learn more advanced design patterns and techniques. This site itself is, in my opinion, a better version of the final cert project. However, from the very beginning I planned on creating a hub to get to know me and my work

The Future...

I have plans for another certification from freeCodeCamp and 2 more fullstack webapps (see WIPS below). They're gonna be a lot more polished, with more functionality, attention to detail and accessibility, and performant. I really love making games too and will definitely look to make a more advanced and original title. No matter what lofty goal I may chase, though, it'll be done out of passion and a love of learning as much as I possibly can.
