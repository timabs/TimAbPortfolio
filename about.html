About Me

Hi, my name is Timur (sometimes also Morse Lane).

I'm a 24 year old from Brooklyn doing whatever it takes to break into my dream field while documenting the whole process.

It started in 2016 (age 16-17) while I was fighting Burkitt's Lymphoma, a rare cancer which forced me into eight months of intensive treatment. Burned skin, "chemobrain" and malnourishment consumed me at a time when I was meant to be preparing for my future. Realistically, all I could do was sleep and stare at screens, or maybe play a video game. Technology allowed me to stay connected to my friends and the rest of the world as well as provide a well-needed distraction. I started college on time, in remission, and declared as a comsci major. I didn't graduate that way, rather I became a polisci major due to bad experiences damaging my perspective on the career outlook. It wasn't until after graduation I realized that, while political science was a valuable degree that could have taken me to a good law school, I'd be so much happier chasing the software engineering fields I still feel so passionate about. So I've decided to work my full time day job, come home, and dedicate almost all my free time to learning the new skills needed to get to where I want to be: making software that's functional, reliable, and pleasant to experience. When my day job becomes my dream job and I can afford it, I want to set up my mom in a better apartment to thank her for leaving her life behind so I might have a better one. I also want to be able to support my disabled partner so that she can pursue what she loves without fear.
